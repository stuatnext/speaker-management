# NEXTPredict 2026 — Speaker Securing Playbook

How to take a name from *Potential* to *on stage*. A repeatable method first,
then tailored angles for the priority targets.

---

## The method (works for any speaker)

### 1. Lead with the audience, not the ask
Senior people say yes to **rooms**, not to favours. Open every approach with who
they'll be in front of and alongside: _"a room of prediction-market operators,
exchange leaders, institutional investors and the press defining this category."_
Name-drop confirmed peers (Jason Robins, Joey Levy, Jeremy Levine) — social proof
closes faster than flattery.

### 2. Make the first ask specific
"Will you speak?" stalls. "Would you headline a 25-minute fireside on whether
event contracts go mainstream in 2026?" converts. Propose **format + topic +
rough date** so they can picture it. You can always flex later.

### 3. Reduce their effort to near zero
Offer to: draft their session title and abstract, pair them with a strong
moderator, and keep prep to one 30-minute call. The easier you make it look, the
faster the yes.

### 4. Create gentle urgency
"We're finalising the prediction-markets track in the next two weeks and I'd love
to hold a headline slot for you." Real scarcity (a closing agenda) beats
manufactured pressure.

### 5. Always use the warmest path in
Cold email is the last resort for senior targets. Check first for:
- a mutual connection (board members, confirmed speakers, investors in common),
- a recent article/podcast/launch you can reference authentically,
- a NEXT.io relationship already in HubSpot.
A one-line warm intro outperforms ten cold sends.

### 6. Follow up like a professional
Three touches, spaced ~4–5 days, each adding value (a new confirmed name, a
relevant agenda detail, a press angle) — never just "bumping this." After three
silent touches, mark **Leave for Now** and move on; revisit next cycle.

## Building a balanced lineup

Beyond individual yeses, the **content team's** job is a lineup that tells a
story. Aim to cover, across the confirmed + target set:

- **Exchanges / platforms** — the operators (Kalshi-type, Robinhood-type).
- **Investors / VCs** — the capital and the thesis (Perkins, Qureshi, Noyes).
- **Institutional / regulated finance** — credibility and the rules (Crighton,
  Cohen).
- **Media / analysts** — the storytellers and skeptics (Brewer, Knibbs, Gouker).
- **Builders / founders** — the product and the bets (Levy, Robins).

If any pillar is thin, that's where the next *Potential* invites should aim.
Diversity of voice (background, gender, geography) is part of a credible lineup —
sense-check the final set against it.
